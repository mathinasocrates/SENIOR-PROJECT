Quick Action Guides

If You Feel an Earthquake

  1. DROP to your hands and knees
  2. TAKE COVER under a desk or table
  3. HOLD ON to your shelter
  4. Stay away from windows and heavy objects
  5. After shaking stops, evacuate if instructed

If Evacuation Order is Given

  1. Grab your emergency kit immediately
  2. Follow designated evacuation routes
  3. Do NOT return for belongings
  4. Head to nearest assembly point
  5. Report to emergency personnel

Evacuation Steps
1
Immediate Response (0-5 minutes)
  • Stay calm and alert
  • Grab pre-packed emergency bag
  • Turn off gas and electricity if time permits
  • Gather family members
2
Leave Home (5-10 minutes)
  • Exit via designated route
  • Help elderly and disabled neighbors
  • Do NOT drive if roads are congested
  • Walk if necessary - stay together
3
Reach Assembly Point (10-30 minutes)
  • Report to emergency personnel
  • Provide headcount of your group
  • Follow instructions from authorities
  • Wait for further evacuation if needed
Important Safety Tips
  • Never return to evacuated areas until officially cleared
  • Cover mouth and nose with cloth if ash is falling
  • Stay on designated routes - do not take shortcuts
  • Help others but don't endanger yourself
Understanding Volcanic Hazards
Primary Hazards
Lava Flows

What: Molten rock flowing from the volcano at temperatures up to 1200°C

Danger: Destroys everything in its path, moves 1-10 km/hour

Protection: Evacuate immediately - lava flows cannot be stopped

Pyroclastic Flows

What: Fast-moving currents of hot gas, ash, and volcanic debris

Danger: Extremely deadly, temperatures up to 800°C, speeds up to 200 km/hour

Protection: Immediate evacuation - cannot be outrun if close

Toxic Gases

What: Carbon dioxide (CO₂), sulfur dioxide (SO₂), hydrogen sulfide (H₂S)

Danger: Suffocation, respiratory problems, poisoning

Protection: Evacuate low-lying areas, use masks, seek higher ground

Secondary Hazards
Volcanic Ash
  • Health: Respiratory problems, eye irritation
  • Infrastructure: Roof collapse, water contamination
  • Protection: Stay indoors, seal windows, use masks
Lahars (Volcanic Mudflows)
  • Formation: Volcanic debris mixed with water
  • Danger: Fast-moving, carries boulders and trees
  • Protection: Avoid valleys and drainage areas
Early Warning Signs
  • Increased earthquake activity
  • Ground deformation and swelling
  • Changes in gas emissions
  • New or increased hot springs
  • Animal behavior changes

Health Protection Guide
Respiratory Protection
Volcanic Ash Dangers

Immediate Effects: Eye, nose, and throat irritation, difficulty breathing

Long-term Effects: Respiratory illness, especially for those with asthma or heart conditions

Most Vulnerable: Children, elderly, pregnant women, people with respiratory conditions

Protecting Your Airways
  • Stay Indoors: Close all windows and doors
  • Use Masks: N95 respirators or multiple layers of cloth
  • Seal Gaps: Use tape around windows and doors
  • Avoid Exercise: Reduces heavy breathing of contaminated air
  • Use AC: Set to recirculate indoor air only
Eye Protection
If ash gets in your eyes:
  • Do NOT rub your eyes
  • Flush with clean water immediately
  • Use protective eyewear when outside
  • Remove contact lenses if irritation occurs
  • Seek medical attention for persistent irritation
Gas Exposure
Dangerous Volcanic Gases
Carbon Dioxide (CO₂):
  • Odorless and colorless
  • Heavier than air (settles in low areas)
  • Can cause suffocation
  • Action: Move to higher ground
Sulfur Dioxide (SO₂):
  • Strong, choking odor
  • Causes breathing difficulties
  • Irritates eyes and throat
  • Action: Move away from source

1
Before Emergency

  • Keep medical records accessible
  • Know location of nearest hospitals
  • Identify medical needs of family members
2
During Emergency
  • Take medications as prescribed
  • Monitor symptoms closely
  • Seek immediate help for breathing difficulties
  • Keep medical supplies accessible
3
After Emergency
  • Continue respiratory protection during cleanup
  • Watch for delayed health effects
  • Seek medical evaluation if symptoms persist
  • Follow public health advisories
Protecting Children
Teaching Children About Volcanic Safety
Age-Appropriate Education
Ages 3-7:
  • Simple safety rules
  • Practice "stop, listen, follow"
  • Learn family meeting places
  • Role-play emergency scenarios
  • Use picture books about safety
Ages 8-17:
  • Understand volcanic hazards
  • Memorize emergency contacts
  • Learn first aid basics
  • Practice evacuation routes
  • Help prepare emergency kits
Special Considerations for Young Children
Infants and Toddlers (0-3 years)
  • Evacuation: Always carry children; don't let them walk
  • Breathing: Cover face with damp cloth during ash fall
  • Comfort: Bring favorite toys/blankets for emotional security
  • Feeding: Extra formula, baby food, and clean water
  • Medical: Children's medications and thermometer
School Emergency Plans
What Parents Should Know
  • Know your child's school evacuation plan
  • Understand school communication methods
  • Keep emergency contact information updated
  • Authorize trusted adults to pick up your child
  • Don't go to school during emergency - follow official instructions
Emotional Support
1
Before Emergency
  • Discuss safety plans calmly and positively
  • Read age-appropriate books about emergencies
  • Practice emergency drills as "safety games"
  • Reassure children that adults are prepared
2
During Emergency
  • Stay calm to help children stay calm
  • Explain what's happening in simple terms
  • Keep family together when possible
  • Maintain routines when safe to do so
3
After Emergency
  • Allow children to express their feelings
  • Watch for signs of stress or anxiety
  • Maintain normal routines as much as possible
  • Seek professional help if needed
Warning Signs of Stress in Children
Behavioral Changes:
  • Increased clinginess
  • Sleep problems or nightmares
  • Loss of appetite
  • Regression (thumb sucking, bedwetting)
Emotional Changes:
  • Increased anxiety or fears
  • Difficulty concentrating
  • Withdrawal from activities
  • Emotional outbursts
